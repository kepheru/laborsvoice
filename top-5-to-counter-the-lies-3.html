Top 5, to Counter the Lies

  1. Discipline
    1. after you qualify, you can't be disciplined for production (over-allowed etc.)
      1. in order to qualify you don't need to make scratch, you only need to show progressive improvement
    2. count your days, management can't be relied upon to accurately do so
      1. they'll come to you saying you've got 29 days, when you really have 32, and if you aren't aware that you've got 32, you would be disqualified
    3. take notes, take notes, take notes!

      1. take notes in your discipline hearings, you'd be surprised how your memory can fail you

      2. take notes daily on what you run while in your packet, this may be instrumental if they try disqualify you

      3. take notes on any instances of harassment or disparate treatment from management

    4. The "cardinal sins" that cause you to be removed from service immediately

      1. anything that compromises your integrity (don't lie, cheat, or steal)

      2. having a serious accident, where the driver is presumed to be at fault. Defined as:

        1. there is a fatality

        2. a citation is issued, and someone is treated away from the scene of accident for bodily injury

        3. a citation is issued, and one or more vehicles is towed from the scene

        4. an accident that involves contact with an aircraft, which grounds that aircraft

  2. Integrity

    1. sheet every package

    2. don't falsify records (improperly sheet anything)

    3. don't say something that could be misrepresented by management later on as dishonest

      1. it's better to say "I'm not sure", or "I don't recall", than to be accused of lying and be removed from service

    4. Don't do anything that could compromise your integrity!

  3. Communication

    1. communicate through the board or by text... No Calls!!!

      1. management will intentionally call you when they are trying to tell you to do shady things. cause they plan on throwing you under the bus immediately, if it comes back to bite them in the ass

    2. save any texts with management

    3. take a picture of messages you send and/or get sent through the board that could be important later on

    4. It's better to communicate any issues you are having, and to communicate early

    5. Put the responsibility for that service failure, or whatever it may be, on them. Take it off of your shoulders by communicating, and letting them make the decision.

  4. Union Rights

    1. always ask for a steward when having a meeting with management

      1. this doesn't just apply to "official" seeming meetings, don't let I'm trap you into a conversation that could lead to Look, when in doubt, ask for your steward

      2. look up your Weingarten Rights

    2. you have to work as instructed, unless instructed to something:

      1. immoral
      2. illegal
      3. unsafe

    3. Once you qualify, you attain seniority

      1. Seniority is the amount of time you've been with the company in a given classification

      2. Management is supposed to take seniority into account when they do things like giving people days off, or assigning routes

      3. You have the right to exercise seniority when they fail to, by bumping people on an easier route than you're assigned to for instance

  5. Safety

    1. you are allowed to refuse to go out without a functional hand cart

    2. you can refuse to deliver over 70s without assistance

      1. think 3rd floor of apartments for example

      2. you do not have to seek that assistance from customers

    3. if you get into an accident, you must report it

      1. it's an immediately dischargeable offense to attempt to hide an accident

      2. it's better to get a warning letter for safety than to get fired

    4. safety trumps everything!

      1. management will pay lip service to safety, and then simultaneously tell you to do some crazy stupid stuff... don't

      2. the safety of the public, and more importantly, your safety, is much more important than over-allowed/under-allowed, or whatever thing might be in those boxes. don't let them ever get you to forget that
